# Technology Areas — Definitions for Team Evaluation

This document defines the **five Technology Areas** used to evaluate NFL teams on the Technology & Fan Experience Scorecard. Share these definitions with teams up front so responses are consistent and scorable.

**Alignment with deck:** The proposal deck frames the scorecard as *“Enabling The Digital Fan & Operations Experience”* and calls out NFL focus areas including **Engagement & Personalization**, **Transaction & Commerce**, and **Immersive Experiences**. Marketing is listed as a key stakeholder (with IT, Business Operations). Including fan-facing marketing—campaigns, offers, loyalty, personalization, commerce—in these definitions is therefore **within the spirit of the deck**: it is part of the fan journey and the technology layers that enable it.

**Evaluation context (from deck):** Capabilities are evaluated across **(1)** the full fan journey (Research & Buy → Transportation → Entry → Food & Beverage → During Game → Restrooms → Merchandise → After Game) and **(2)** the three game phases—Pre-Game, In-Game, Post-Game—with tech layers weighted by impact to each phase where relevant.

---

## 1. Technology Infrastructure

**What it covers:** Foundational physical and network infrastructure required to deliver fan and venue experiences. Focus is on *connectivity, compute, and core systems* that other capabilities depend on.

**Include when evaluating:**
- Venue Wi‑Fi (guest, staff, operations, broadcast)
- Wired LAN and backbone
- Circuits, backhaul, and carrier connectivity
- Neutral Host DAS (NH‑DAS) and cellular in-venue
- Cloud hosting and edge compute used for venue/fan systems
- Camera and video ingest networks
- Core integration layer (APIs, middleware that ties systems together)
- Network and system monitoring/observability
- Data center or on-prem hosting for venue systems
- Power, cooling, and physical plant that supports technology

**Out of scope:** Business applications (e.g. HR, finance). Back-office-only tools (e.g. internal brand asset management) that do not directly support fan/venue experiences. *Fan-facing marketing tech (campaigns, offers, loyalty) is in scope under Digital Services and Technology Services.*

**Evaluation lens:** *Does the team have the right infrastructure in place to support reliable, scalable fan and operational experiences?*

---

## 2. Technology Services

**What it covers:** Technology-powered services that support **business and venue operations**—how the organization runs and supports technology, not the fan-facing product itself.

**Include when evaluating:**
- **Broadcast and 5G/connectivity services** — broadcast technology operations, 5G and carrier services, and connectivity services that support fan/venue experiences (per deck: “Broadcast, 5G services, etc”)
- IT service management (ITSM), ticketing, change management
- Vendor management, SLAs, and technology operations
- Security operations (SOC, monitoring, incident response)
- **Marketing and fan engagement technology operations** — ad servers, campaign management, email/push/SMS platforms, loyalty and CRM ops that power fan-facing marketing and promotions (part of fan experience)
- Support and help desk for staff and partners
- Technology governance, standards, and lifecycle management
- Training and enablement for staff on technology
- Sourcing, procurement, and contract management for technology

**Out of scope:** The fan-facing digital products (those sit under Digital Services). Infrastructure hardware/network (those sit under Technology Infrastructure).

**Evaluation lens:** *How well does the team operate, support, and govern technology across the organization and venue?*

---

## 3. Digital Infrastructure

**What it covers:** **Core platforms and data/identity layers** that underpin digital services—the “plumbing” that makes apps, integrations, and data-driven experiences possible.

**Include when evaluating:**
- Broadband networks and wide-area connectivity that underpin digital experiences (per deck: “Broadband networks, cloud hosting, etc”)
- Cloud hosting and platform services for apps and data (distinct from venue-specific edge/infra in Technology Infrastructure)
- Data pipelines, data lakes, and event streaming
- APIs, data contracts, and integration platforms
- Identity and access management (IAM) for fans, staff, and partners
- Customer data platform (CDP) or unified fan data
- Observability, logging, and APM for digital systems
- Developer platforms and internal tooling for building digital experiences
- Content management and delivery (CMS, CDN) that powers digital touchpoints
- Event-driven and real-time data infrastructure

**Out of scope:** End-user apps and touchpoints (Digital Services). Physical network/server infrastructure (Technology Infrastructure).

**Evaluation lens:** *Does the team have the right data, identity, and platform capabilities to build and run modern digital experiences?*

---

## 4. Digital Services

**What it covers:** **Fan- and staff-facing digital products and experiences** delivered via apps, web, signage, and kiosks. Focus is on what users interact with and the capabilities those experiences provide.

**Include when evaluating:**
- Stadium mobile app (features, usability, integration with venue)
- Digital ticketing, mobile tickets, and entry experiences (e.g. NFC, QR)
- Mobile ordering, pickup, delivery, and in-seat/concession commerce
- Wayfinding, maps, and in-venue navigation
- Digital signage and in-stadium displays
- Kiosks (ticketing, concession, info)
- **Immersive experiences** (AR, VR, mixed reality)—e.g. seat previews, activations (deck focus area: “Immersive Experiences”)
- In-stadium commerce, loyalty, and personalized offers
- **Fan-facing marketing and promotions** — in-app and in-venue campaigns, pre-game and game-day offers, loyalty and rewards experiences, sponsor/partner activations, email/push/SMS that drive fan engagement (marketing as part of the fan journey)
- Broadcast and media-related fan digital experiences (where they are fan-facing)
- Staff-facing apps and tools used to run the game-day experience

**Out of scope:** Back-office business applications. Internal-only marketing workflow (e.g. creative approval) that doesn’t touch the fan experience. Infrastructure or platform layers (those are Technology Infrastructure / Digital Infrastructure).

**Evaluation lens:** *How well do the team’s digital products and touchpoints serve fans and staff across the fan journey?*

---

## 5. AI & Innovation

**What it covers:** **AI/ML and emerging technology** used to augment the fan experience, venue operations, and business outcomes. Includes both deployed capabilities and experimentation.

**Include when evaluating:**
- Computer vision (crowd density, queue detection, safety, analytics)
- Predictive analytics (egress, demand, staffing, inventory)
- Personalization and recommendation engines (content, offers, experiences)
- NLP/chatbots and agentic assistants (fan support, internal tools)
- Experimentation and A/B testing frameworks
- Gen AI use cases (content, summarization, search, assistants)
- IoT and sensor-driven analytics (parking, concourse, restrooms)
- **Sustainability and innovation** — technology for energy management, carbon/impact tracking, and green/venue sustainability when tied to fan/venue operations (deck focus area: “Sustainability & Innovation”)
- Innovation lab, pilots, and proof-of-concepts that are tied to fan/venue impact

**Out of scope:** Standard business intelligence or reporting without ML. Generic “innovation” that is not technology-led or not tied to fan/venue outcomes.

**Evaluation lens:** *How effectively does the team use AI and emerging tech to improve fan experience, safety, and operations?*

## Summary table (for quick reference)

| Area | One-line scope | Evaluation focus |
|------|----------------|------------------|
| **Technology Infrastructure** | Physical + network + compute that underpin experiences | Reliability, scale, and readiness of core infrastructure |
| **Technology Services** | Broadcast, 5G/connectivity services; ops, governance, support | How well technology and connectivity services are run and supported |
| **Digital Infrastructure** | Broadband, cloud, data, identity, APIs, and platforms | Readiness to build and run modern digital experiences |
| **Digital Services** | Apps, web, signage, kiosks, immersive experiences—what fans and staff use | Quality and coverage of digital touchpoints |
| **AI & Innovation** | AI/ML and emerging tech for fan/venue impact | Use of AI and innovation to improve outcomes |

## Marketing and fan experience

**Marketing is part of the fan experience** when it touches the fan journey: pre-game campaigns, in-venue offers, loyalty, sponsor activations, and personalized engagement. We do *not* exclude it.

- **Digital Services** — Fan-*facing* marketing: the experiences fans see and interact with (campaigns, offers, loyalty, activations in app/signage/email/push).
- **Technology Services** — Marketing *operations*: the platforms and ops that run campaigns, ad serving, and fan engagement tech.
- **Digital Infrastructure** — Data and identity that enable personalization and targeting (CDP, segments).
- **AI & Innovation** — Personalization and recommendation engines that power tailored offers and content.

What we *exclude* is marketing that never touches the fan: e.g. internal brand asset management, creative workflow, or reporting that doesn’t affect the experience. Those stay out of scope for this scorecard.

---

## How to use with the scorecard

- **Survey design:** Map each question to exactly one Technology Area; use these definitions to avoid overlap or ambiguity. Consider impact across the three game phases (Pre-Game, In-Game, Post-Game) and the fan journey where relevant.
- **Team alignment:** Share this document (or a one-pager derived from it) with teams before they respond so everyone uses the same scope.
- **Scoring:** When interpreting responses, use the “Evaluation lens” and “Include when evaluating” to decide if a capability belongs in that area and how to score it.
